DATA EXPLORATION AND VISUALIZATION

1. Data Profiling
    - No. of rows after data cleaning: 1000 
    - No. of columns: 13
    - Data Integrity: All duplicates were removed, and null values were handled during the initial cleaning phase in Excel.

2. Income & Gender Analysis
    - Actions:
       - Created a pivot table to compare the average income of purchasers vs. non-purchasers, segmented by gender.
       - Then, used a clustered bar chart to compare the results. 

    - Key Insights: 
       - Customers who purchased a bike consistently show a higher average income compared to those who didn’t.
       - In the given dataset, Male customers have higher average income than Female customers in both bike ‘purchaser’ and ‘non-purchaser’ categories.

3. Commute Distance vs. Bike Purchase Analysis
    - Actions:
       - Analyzed purchase volume against commute distance using a smooth line chart. 
       - Then, used a smooth line chart for visualization. 
 
    - Key Insights:
       - It is evident that customers with the shortest commute distances have the highest bike sales volume, suggesting bikes are primarily used 
          for short-distance commuting rather than long-distance travel.
       - Bike sales drop off significantly as commute distance increases, especially at the "More than 10 Miles" mark.
       - The number of bike purchasers in that mark is 2x smaller than non-purchasers, indicating that most people in that demographic are not interested 
          in the product, except perhaps for those who use it for exercise.

4. Age Demographic Analysis
    - Actions:
       - Categorized ages into "Young Adult," "Middle Age," and "Senior" brackets.
       - Generated a pivot table utilizing the age brackets and determined the bike volume purchased per age bracket.
       - Then, used a line chart for visualization. 

    - Key Insights:
       - It is conspicuously clear that the Middle Age demographic dominates the dataset. With 383 bike purchases, this group outperforms Young Adults by 
          nearly 10x and Seniors by over 6x in total sales volume.

5. Bike Purchases by Region
    - Actions:
       - Horizontal bar chart comparing sales volume across North America, Europe, and the Pacific.

    - Key Insights:
       - While North America has the highest volume of customers, the Pacific region shows the highest conversion rate, with more people buying bikes than not.
